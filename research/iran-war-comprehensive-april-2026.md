## 1. Current Status & Ceasefire

**Source:** Google AI Mode Search (April 22, 2026)

A fragile ceasefire is currently in place as of April 22, 2026, but under significant strain due to ongoing military actions and diplomatic deadlock.

### Key Developments:
- **Ceasefire Extension:** US President Donald Trump has indefinitely extended the ceasefire with Iran, originally set to expire on Wednesday (April 22). The extension was requested by Pakistani mediators to allow the "fractured" Iranian government more time to submit a unified proposal for a permanent end to the war.
- **Ongoing Blockade:** Despite the truce, the US naval blockade of Iranian ports remains in full effect. Iran considers this blockade a direct violation of the ceasefire and an "act of war."
- **Strait of Hormuz Attack (April 22):** An IRGC gunboat fired on a container ship in the Strait of Hormuz, causing heavy damage to the bridge but no casualties.
- **Israeli Strikes:** Despite a separate 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah, Israeli drone strikes in Lebanon's Bekaa region killed one person and wounded two others.
- **Hezbollah Retaliation (April 21):** Hezbollah launched rockets and attack drones into northern Israel, citing "blatant" Israeli violations of the truce as justification.

### Casualty Figures:
Since the war began, over 3,375 people have been killed in Iran and more than 2,290 in Lebanon (per Associated Press). Lebanon's disaster management unit reports death toll from weeks of Israeli attacks at 2,454 with 7,658 injured.

### Diplomatic Status:
Negotiations in Islamabad, Pakistan are currently in limbo. Vice President J.D. Vance's planned trip was put on hold after Iran rebuffed immediate efforts to restart talks. The Strait of Hormuz remains effectively shut as Iran insists it will not reopen while the US blockade continues.
